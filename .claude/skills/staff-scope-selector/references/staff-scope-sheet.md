# Staff Scope Sheet

Detail for `staff-scope-selector`. Read on demand.

## Staff archetypes (Larson/Cutler-style)

| Archetype | Leverage |
|---|---|
| Tech Lead | Guides a team's execution on the most important thing |
| Architect | Owns direction/quality of a critical area over time |
| Solver | Parachutes into the hardest problem and cracks it |
| Right Hand | Extends leadership's reach on org-wide priorities |

Different archetypes fit different people and orgs — match to strength +
need.

## Leverage vs busy-ness test

Ask of any candidate scope:
- Does it **multiply** other engineers (unblock, enable, set direction)?
- Would the org **notice its absence**?
- Is it the **important** problem or just the **urgent** one?
- Does it move something only someone at this level can?

If it's just more/harder individual output → senior work, not staff
leverage.

## Trap catalog

| Trap | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Only-fun | Shiny tech, low impact | Pick boring-high-impact over exciting-low-impact |
| Only-firefighting | Reactive all quarter | Fix the systemic cause; set direction |
| Too narrow | Well-built feature, senior-level | Raise ambition to the level |
| Invisible glue | High value, no credit | Keep it; add visibility/attribution |
| Over-reach | Taking others' owned scope | Align first; fill gaps instead |

## Scope-rationale format

```
CHOSEN: <scope>
Leverage type: multiplier | direction | hard-problem | glue
Fit: <the strength this draws on>
Org need: <the important under-owned problem it addresses>
Why this over alternatives: <reasoning>
NOT doing: <explicit non-scope — focus is real>
Visibility: <how impact will be attributable>
```

## Under-owned problem hunting

The best staff scope is often:
- The gap BETWEEN teams nobody owns.
- The decision nobody is making.
- The system everyone complains about but no one drives.
- The recurring class of incident nobody has rooted out.

Owning everything = owning nothing. Choose, and say no to the rest.
